FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Eligibility

Healthy individuals aged 18-65, weighing at least 45kg, with no recent illnesses, tattoos, or piercings, and no high-risk behaviors, can donate. A hemoglobin test is required to ensure eligibility.

Donation Process

Whole blood can be donated every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet or plasma donations may allow more frequent donations (every 7-14 days) depending on your health and donation type, as advised by a doctor.

Whole blood donation takes about 15-30 minutes, including pre-donation screening. Platelet donation can take 45-60 minutes, or up to 2-3 hours including screening.

Your blood is tested for infectious diseases, separated into components (red cells, plasma, platelets), and stored under specific conditions. It’s then distributed to hospitals for patients in need.

Health and Safety

The process involves a small needle prick, which may cause mild discomfort. The donation itself is painless, and any soreness usually subsides within a day. Rest and hydration help recovery.

Rest for 10-15 minutes, drink plenty of water, eat a snack, and avoid heavy lifting for the rest of the day. Contact a healthcare provider if you feel dizzy or unwell.

Donating blood is generally safe and can even stimulate new blood cell production. However, some people may feel lightheaded. Following post-donation care instructions helps minimize any effects.

Inform the staff immediately if you feel faint. They’ll have you lie down and provide care. After donation, rest, hydrate, and avoid strenuous activity to prevent fainting.
